My Journey

To code, or not to code, that is the question.

Well, at least it was a question until recently. These days, having some understanding of computer languages is a requirement in so many industries that understanding how to code is becoming a fundamental skill, like math or writing.

With these changes in mind, I decided to put my eggs in the basket of the software developer. After a few courses in computer science at Virginia Tech's College of Engineering, I fell in love with the craft. Finally, my nit-pickiness, tendency to over-optimize, and childlike curiosity could become an asset rather than a liability!

As an undergraduate I was fortunate enough to land three internships:

ATEC Wireless: Developed a WPF-solution to an RF-signal decoding problem using C# and XAML
Lutron Electronics: Developed a Computer-Vision feature to automatically mark all symbols on any floor plan. Used the MVVM design pattern with C#, XAML, and SQL
Capital One: Developed a data pipeline using Spark/Scala.

Currently, I am happily employed by Capital One. I work in their Capital Markets technology department in New York City as a software engineer.
