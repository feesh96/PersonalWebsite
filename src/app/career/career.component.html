My Journey

Always curious, I like to think my career truly began when I used my first desktop computer. At the time, pixels, the internet, CPUs, and monitors were magic to me, and I quickly fell in love with technology. My father is an electrical engineer by trade and inspired me to follow my curiosity and pursue a career in computing.

During my tenure at Virginia Tech, I was fortunate enough to land three internships: First, I worked with ATEC Wireless to develop a WPF-solution to an RF-signal decoding problem using C# and XAML. Next, I developed a Computer-Vision feature to automatically mark all symbols on any floor plan for Lutron Electronics, using the MVVM design pattern with C#, XAML, and SQL. This led to my final internship with Capital One where I was tasked with fully integrating into a full-time team to build a complex data pipeline.

The work was challenging and exciting enough to bring me back for a full time position in Capital One's New York office, where I currently work in the Capital Markets Technology department.
